What Personal Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following types of personal data:

  • Contact information (name, email, company name, job title)
  • Company information (industry, team size, tools currently used)
  • Interaction data (your responses to our agent, demo form inputs, use preferences)
  • Behavioral data (pages visited, time on site, agent drop-off points - if cookies are consented)

Data Collected by Our Virtual Assistant "Claudia"

Our virtual agent Claudia may engage with you on our website to:

  • Answer questions about our product
  • Collect feedback on your needs and preferences
  • Offer early access to demos, pilots, or test panels
  • Help you register for our waiting list

Agent-specific data includes:

  • Your answers to optional questions (e.g., current tools, pain points)
  • Information you voluntarily provide to join a test panel or receive updates
  • Consent preferences (if opted into communications or data usage)

All interactions are logged for internal product improvement, but will not be used for automated decision-making that affects your rights or obligations.

You are free to end the conversation at any time and may request deletion of your conversation history (see Your Rights section).

How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data to:

  • Understand your needs and improve our offering
  • Respond to inquiries or requests for a demo
  • Manage participation in early-access or test programs
  • Send updates (only if you opted in)
  • Ensure security and functionality of our services
  • Meet legal or regulatory requirements

We do not sell your data to third parties.

Legal Grounds for Processing

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Consent — when you voluntarily submit information, such as joining the test panel, requesting a demo, or interacting with our AI tools.
  • Contractual necessity — to provide access to prototypes or services you've requested.
  • Legitimate interest — to ensure platform security, troubleshoot issues, and improve our services (never for advertising or profiling).

You may withdraw your consent at any time. See the Your Rights section for details.

Cookies and Tracking

Privacy-friendly by design. We do not use tracking cookies or third-party advertising technologies.

Some of our technical infrastructure (e.g., Vercel for hosting, Cloud Run for backend services) may generate basic system logs or use essential cookies to ensure performance and security.

These do not track you across sites or create user profiles.

If we introduce any non-essential cookies or analytics tools in the future, we will request your explicit consent before activating them.

Data Retention

We keep your data:

  • For up to 12 months after your last interaction, unless a longer retention is required for legal reasons
  • Test panel feedback and demo requests are retained for up to 18 months to support product development

Your Rights Under GDPR

As a user of our website and services, you have important rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have control over your personal data.

1. Access Your Data

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, including how we use it and why.

2. Request a Correction

If any of the personal data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you can ask us to update or correct it.

3. Request Deletion ("Right to Be Forgotten")

You may request the deletion of your personal data at any time, especially if:

  • It's no longer needed for the purpose we collected it for
  • You withdraw your consent
  • You object to its use

4. Withdraw Consent

If you've given us consent to use your data (e.g. to receive demo updates, join the test panel, or interact with our virtual agent), you can withdraw that consent at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of any use prior to withdrawal.

5. File a Complaint

If you believe your data rights have been violated, you can file a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority. In the Netherlands, this is the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (www.autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl).

How to Make a Request

Data Security

We take appropriate security measures to protect your data, including:

  • Encrypted form submissions: Form data is transmitted securely over encrypted (HTTPS) connections.
  • Role-based access to stored information: Access to form submissions is restricted to authorized team members only.

International Transfers

Some data may be processed outside the European Economic Area (EEA) via third-party service providers (e.g. hosting or cloud tools). When this occurs, we rely on safeguards such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) to ensure adequate protection of your data in line with GDPR.
